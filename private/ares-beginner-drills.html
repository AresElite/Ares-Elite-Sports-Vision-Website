How this program works

Read this once, then live in the schedule.

This program takes the eight core vision drills and spreads them across 28 days. Each day you do two drills — a different pairing every day — so no single system gets overworked and every drill is trained evenly. Each drill appears seven times across the four weeks.

Daily rhythm

  • Two drills a day, 10-15 minutes total
  • Do them in either order
  • Six days on, one rest day — or all seven if you feel good
  • Quality over speed: controlled beats fast

What you need

  • A pencil with a sharp tip
  • A window with a long-distance view
  • Somewhere quiet to sit or stand
  • That's genuinely it

If your eyes ache, stop

Mild fatigue is normal in the first week — it means the muscles are working. Sharp pain, headache, or doubled vision that does not resolve is not normal. Stop the drill and talk to your eye care provider before continuing.

Track it or lose it

Tap each day in the schedule as you finish it. Your progress saves in this browser, so the program remembers where you left off when you come back.

The drills

Drill library

Purpose, setup, and prescription for all eight.

Near-Far Jumps (Level 1)

None (a dry-erase marker helps)
Purpose
To develop strength in the focusing system (the accommodative system), which drives your ability to jump back and forth between distance and near objects efficiently.
How to do it
  1. Stand in front of a window with a long-distance view, about 18-20 inches from the glass.
  2. Cover your left eye first, keeping your right eye open.
  3. Find the furthest object you can see in the distance — this is your DISTANCE target.
  4. Pick a speck of dirt on the glass (or place a dry-erase mark) — this is your NEAR target.
  5. Shift focus back and forth between the two, holding each for 5 seconds.
Complete 25 cycles (1 cycle = distance → near → distance)

Eye Circles

None
Purpose
To build smooth, controlled range of motion through the full orbit of eye movement.
How to do it
  1. Keep your eyes open and move them in a clockwise direction.
  2. If this feels uncomfortable, close your eyes and perform the same movement.
  3. Circle the eyes around 20 times.
  4. Repeat, circling in the counterclockwise direction.
Complete 20 circles clockwise + 20 counterclockwise

Figure 8's Drill

Pencil
Purpose
To train the system that controls how your eyes move together while following an object — your Smooth Pursuits.
How to do it
  1. Hold a pencil out at arm's length in front of you.
  2. Make a figure-8 design in the air with the pencil.
  3. Without moving your head — using only your eyes — follow the pencil tip through the air.
  4. Move the pencil slowly at first, tracing a small figure 8.
  5. As you get more proficient, increase both the speed and the size of the figure 8.
Complete 15 figure 8's clockwise + 15 counterclockwise

ABC's (Level 1)

None
Purpose
To move your eyes together quickly and efficiently, strengthening the system as a whole for both strength and quickness.
How to do it
  1. Stare straight ahead.
  2. Beginning with the letter A, trace the letter using only your eyes.
  3. Keep your head straight at all times and draw an exaggerated, large version of the letter.
  4. Repeat with every letter of the alphabet, from A through Z.
Complete 1 complete cycle through the capital alphabet

Eye Contractions

None
Purpose
To build control of the muscles that close and open the eye, and to flush strain through rapid blinking.
How to do it
  1. Close your eyes as tightly as possible and hold for 4 seconds.
  2. Open your eyes and blink as quickly as possible 15 times.
  3. Rest briefly, then repeat.
Complete 5 sets

Superior Oblique Muscles

None
Purpose
To strengthen the muscles of the eye to improve control and function through diagonal movement patterns.
How to do it
  1. Sit or stand in a comfortable position.
  2. Trace an invisible figure 8 along the floor about 8-10 feet in front of you — without moving your head.
  3. Trace the lines in one direction, then switch and trace the other direction.
Complete 3-5 sets

Pencil Push-Ups

Pencil
Purpose
To train the part of your vision that moves your eyes in opposite directions at the same time — Convergence (in) and Divergence (out).
How to do it
  1. Hold a pencil out at arm's length in front of you.
  2. Focusing on the tip of the sharpened edge, slowly bring the pencil toward your nose.
  3. When you see 2 pencil tips (your vision has split), push it back out slightly until you see 1 again.
  4. Focus on the tip for 10 seconds, then push it back out to arm's length.
Complete 15 cycles (in from arm's length → hold 10 sec → back out)

Eye Massage (Superior Rectus)

None
Purpose
To relieve strain and stress while massaging the muscles that surround and support the eyeball, keeping them stretched and limber.
How to do it
  1. Find a comfortable position and close your eyes.
  2. Place your thumbs gently below the eyebrows, at the inside corners of the eyes near the nose.
  3. Rest your other four fingers on your forehead.
  4. Apply gentle inward pressure with the thumbs and count to four.
  5. Using the index finger and thumb, massage the bridge of the nose, counting to four as pressure is applied.
  6. Repeat the sequence four times.
Complete 3-5 minutes per day

What good progress looks like

Most athletes see their near point of convergence shorten by two to four inches over four weeks, and their alphabet time drop by a quarter or more. If nothing moves in four weeks, that is useful information too — bring it to a vision exam.
